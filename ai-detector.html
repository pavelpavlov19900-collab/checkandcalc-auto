How this free AI text detector works

HumanScore is a lightweight AI content checker that gives you a quick first impression of whether a text looks more human or machine-like. Instead of trying to guess the exact model (GPT, Claude, etc.), it focuses on simple, transparent signals such as sentence length, vocabulary variety and repetition. These patterns are often different in AI-generated content compared to natural human writing.

The detector calculates an AI-likeness score between 0 and 100. Lower scores usually mean the text looks more human-like, while higher scores indicate that the text has more machine-like characteristics. The score is heuristic and can be wrong, but it helps you decide when to look closer at a piece of content.

What signals does the AI content detector use?

This free AI detector does not send your text to a remote server. It runs directly in your browser and uses a few basic statistics:

  • Average sentence length – very long, uniform sentences often appear in AI-generated essays and articles.
  • Vocabulary diversity – the ratio between unique words and total words. Lower diversity can signal repetitive, templated writing.
  • Text length – extremely long and consistently structured content may be a sign of AI assistance.
  • Small random noise – a bit of randomness is added to avoid giving a false sense of absolute precision.

None of these signals alone can reliably prove whether a text was written by a human or by AI. They simply highlight patterns that are statistically common in machine-generated content.

Limitations of AI detectors and why you should be careful

AI detectors – including this simple online AI text checker – are not perfect. Language models evolve quickly, and human writers can also mimic AI-like patterns. On the other hand, people using AI tools can post-edit the result so that it looks more natural and diverse.

Because of this, you should never punish or accuse someone based only on an AI content score. Use detectors as one small signal among many: your knowledge of the person, writing history, plagiarism checks and the context in which the text appears.

FAQ: questions about the AI detector

Is this AI text detector accurate enough for exams or legal decisions?

No. This is a heuristic tool designed for quick checks and curiosity, not for legal or disciplinary decisions. It can highlight suspicious patterns, but it cannot prove that a text was generated by AI. Always use multiple sources of evidence.

Does this AI detector send my text to a server?

No. The current version runs entirely in your browser using JavaScript. The AI-likeness score is calculated locally. That means your text is not stored or transmitted to a remote server by this tool.

Can I use this AI detector for long texts?

Yes, but very long texts may take a bit more time to process in the browser. For best results, you can analyze one section at a time, for example 3–8 paragraphs. That often gives a clearer picture than analyzing a whole book or thesis at once.

What does a high AI-likeness score mean?

A high score means that the text has several machine-like characteristics based on the simple metrics we use. It is a reason to look closer, but not proof of AI authorship. Human writers can also produce repetitive or templated content that looks “AI-ish”.
